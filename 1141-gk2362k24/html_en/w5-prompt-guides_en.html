Prompt Engineering

The Four Core Principles of Prompt Engineering

Four Principles of Prompt Engineering

Principle In plain words
1. Role Who do you want the AI to be?
2. Goal What task do you want the AI to complete?
3. Output Format What should the response look like?
4. Constraints What are the rules and boundaries?

Principle 1: Role

Assign a specific identity to the AI (e.g., editor, analyst) to generate more targeted and context-aware responses.

Principle 2: Goal

Describe the desired outcome and style in a specific and detailed way. Don't just say "write a poem," but "write a short inspirational poem in a specific style."

Principle 3: Output Format

Clearly define the desired structure of the response (e.g., a list, comma-separated, or in markdown format).

Principle 4: Constraints

Set clear rules, explaining "what to do" rather than "what not to do," and specify the length (e.g., at most 50 words).

Discussion Case: 4 Bad Prompts

Case 1 "Help me make a presentation about generative AI."
Further thinking: What is the goal? "How many pages, for whom, what decision to make"? What output format/directory structure is needed? Are there any restrictions on fabrication or data sources? Are examples or scoring rubrics needed to calibrate quality?

Case 2 "Help me make my resume more impressive."
Further thinking: What job/industry are you applying for? What does the other party value? What is your specialty? What is the desired tone/word count/layout? Do you need to quantify your achievements?

Case 3 "Help me create a one-week study plan."
Further thinking: What is your exam date, subject weights, and weaknesses? To what granularity should the plan be (daily/hourly), and what is the success criterion? Should it include a checklist and backup plans (for temporary work/illness)?

Case 4 "Help me write a copy to promote our club activity."
Further thinking: Is the audience freshmen/current students/off-campus? Is the platform IG/club board/poster? Is the goal the number of registrations/attendees/likes and shares? Is there a CTA and deadline? Are multiple versions for A/B testing and scheduling suggestions needed?

Advanced Prompting Techniques

Strengthen the core principles and take it to the next level

Emotion Prompting

Add emotional language to your prompts to leverage the AI's emotionally rich training data, thereby improving accuracy and response quality.

"This is very important for my career, please be sure to answer accurately."

Emotional prompting can strengthen your goal and fine-tune the role the AI plays.

Few-shot Prompting

Provide one or more "input/output" examples in the prompt to have the model imitate the pattern to complete the task.

Re-reading

Finally, ask the model to re-read the prompt before answering to ensure that no key details are missed.

"Re-reading" ensures that the AI perfectly adheres to the goal and scope constraints you set.

Key Takeaways

  • Four Core Principles: Always define the role, goal, format, and constraints.
  • Advanced 1 (Emotional Prompting): Add emotion to increase focus.
  • Advanced 2 (Few-shot): Show, don't just tell, the format you want.
  • Advanced 3 (Re-reading): Ask the AI to double-check your instructions.
