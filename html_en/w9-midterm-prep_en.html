AI Presentation Midterm: Introduction

Week 9: Preparation Phase

Announcement: 11/11 Midterm Presentation Content

Using the "Prompt Engineering" and "AI Presentation Tools (such as Canva)" learned in the previous semester, work in a team collaboration mode to complete a professional 10-minute presentation on a self-selected topic, followed by a 5-minute Q&A session with other groups.

[This Week's Goals]

  • Group formation and role introduction
  • Topic selection and task division
  • First team planning sprint

[Roll Call, Initial Roles, Balanced Grouping]

Explorer

Core Positioning: Direction & Depth

Specific Project Tasks:

  • Define Research Direction: Determine the "unique perspective" the team will take on the topic.
  • In-Depth Data Mining: [Primary Responsibility] Find "academic cases and literature support."
  • Refine Core Questions: [Primary Responsibility] Design the "core concepts and key questions" to be presented at the end.
  • Draft the in-depth content for the "Detailed Introduction."

Implementer

Core Positioning: Output & Quality

Specific Project Tasks:

  • Presentation Visualization: [Primary Responsibility] Use tools like Canva to create all presentation slides.
  • Content Implementation: [Primary Responsibility] Transform the "text drafts" from the Explorer and Spokesperson into precise, easy-to-understand "detailed introduction" graphics and text.
  • Quality Control: Check for typos, layout consistency, and image resolution in the presentation.
  • Integrate all AI-generated content (text, images).

Spokesperson

Core Positioning: Packaging & Presentation

Specific Project Tasks:

  • Script Writing: [Primary Responsibility] Write the "Topic Introduction" and opening statements for "Why It Matters."
  • Storyline Packaging: Transform rigid data into an engaging storyline.
  • Presentation Practice & Delivery: [Primary Responsibility] Deliver the 10-minute oral presentation.
  • Serve as one of the primary responders in Q&A.

Navigator

Core Positioning: Process & Integration

Specific Project Tasks:

  • Develop timeline and progress control.
  • Integrated Fact-Checking: [Primary Responsibility] Verify the accuracy of literature sources cited by the "Explorer" and key data proposed by the "Spokesperson."
  • Ensure Content Consistency: Check that the logic of the presentation's "opening (importance)," "middle section (details)," and "conclusion (core questions)" is coherent.
  • Moderate Q&A: Serve as the moderator, assigning questions to team members for response.

[Topic Selection]

A. Mental Health & Self-Growth

1. College Students' "Imposter Syndrome": Explore its causes and its impact on academic and social life, along with coping strategies.

2. The Science of "Flow": How can AI tools (such as Pomodoro apps and focus software) help create the optimal personal learning and work experience?

3. Sleep Science and Dreams: Explore how sleep quality affects college students' memory, emotional stability, and learning performance.

4. How Cognitive Biases Control Your Decisions: Analyze 1-2 common biases (such as confirmation bias) and how they affect our interpretation of news or consumption behavior.

5. "Self-Identity" in the Digital Age: The tension and anxiety between online personas and authentic selves.

B. Social Care & Social Work

6. How AI Assists Vulnerable Groups: Using "elderly" or "people with disabilities" as examples of smart care applications, explore their convenience and ethical implications.

7. The Weight of "Social Stigma": Explore how stigmatization exacerbates the plight of people with mental illness or those in poverty.

8. "Helpers'" Professional Burnout: Analyze sources of stress and self-care in professions such as social work, nursing, and counseling.

9. What is "Community Building": Explore how local connections (such as community shared spaces and local NGOs) enhance residents' sense of well-being and belonging.

10. Stray Animals' "TNR" and "Zero-Euthanasia" Policies: Explore the balance between social work (animal welfare) and public health.

C. Healthy Eating & Nutrition Science

11. The "Gut-Brain Axis": How does diet affect your emotions, stress, and mental health through gut bacteria?

12. Scientific Validation of Popular Diets: Using "16:8 Intermittent Fasting" or "Ketogenic Diet" as examples, analyze their effectiveness and potential risks.

13. The Battle Between "Plant-Based Meat" and "Cultured Meat": Are these really healthier, more sustainable future food choices?

14. Marketing Traps of "Health Foods": Analyze how nutrition labels and advertising terminology (such as organic, low-fat, gluten-free) affect consumer perception.

15. College Students' (Dining Out/Dormitory) "Dietary Poverty": How to practice a balanced diet with limited budget and choices?

D. Gender & Interpersonal Relationships

16. The "Gamification" of Dating Apps: Does the swipe mechanism expand the social circle or deepen fast-food romance and loneliness?

17. How Media Shapes Our "Romantic Scripts": Analyze how idol dramas or movies influence our expectations of ideal partners.

18. "Emotional Labor": In intimate relationships, friendships, or service industries, who bears more invisible burden?

19. "Friendship" in the Digital Age: Explore how online relationships (online friends, social media likes) impact and supplement real-life connections.

20. Gender Communication Myths: Do men and women really communicate from different planets? (Explore stereotypes vs. socialization)
